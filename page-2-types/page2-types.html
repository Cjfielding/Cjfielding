Home types Keeping as pets FAQ's


Because there are so many jumping spiders I will only name the top eight most common pet species

8 types of jumping spider commonly kept as pets:


1.

Phidippus Regius (Regal Jumping Spider)


Facts about the Phidippus Regius (Regal Jumping Spider)

Distribution - West Indies, southeastern United States
Habitat - Woodland and fields
Breeding Season - March-September
Diet - Insects
Lifespan - 10 months

2.

Platycryptus Undatus (Tan Jumping Spider)


Facts about the Platycryptus Undatus (Tan Jumping Spider)

Distribution - Central and North America
Habitat - Vertical surfaces like walls, fences, and tree trunks
Breeding Season - March-September
Diet - Smaller spiders, Insects
predator - Birds, wasps, reptiles, and big mammals Lifespan - Around 1 year

3.

Phidippus Audax (Bold Jumping Spider)


Facts about the Phidippus Audax (Bold Jumping Spider)

Also Known as - Daring jumping spider, bold jumper
Distribution - United States, Southern Canada, certain regions of northern Mexico, Nicobar Islands, Hawaii and Cuba
Habitat - Open grassland, sometimes found in wall and fence
Diet - Boll weevils, pink bollworms, tarnished plant bugs, and tobacco budworms and smaller spiders
predator - Lizards, birds, and dragonflies
Lifespan - 1-2 years

4.

Salticus Scenicus (Zebra Jumping Spider)


Facts about the Salticus Scenicus (Zebra Jumping Spider)

Distribution - Found throughout the Holarctic, but widespread across Britain, Europe, and North America
Habitat -
  • Outdoors: they are found on the walls, plants, and trees, fences, especially on sunny days
  • Indoors: they can be located on window sill corners behind curtains
Diet - Insects, terrestrial and non-insect arthropods
predator - Spider wasps and mantises are the known predators
Lifespan - Typical longevity in the wild is 1 to 2 years, whereas, in captivity, it is around 2-3

5.

Chrysilla Lauta (Elegant Golden Jumping Spider)


Facts about the Chrysilla Lauta (Elegant Golden Jumping Spider)

Distribution - Predominantly in asia, from Myanmar to China and Vietnam. Have been seen in Sri Lanka, the US and some parts of Europe (most likely as an introduced species)
Habitat - Rain forests, low vegetation.
Diet - feeder insects like crickets, flies, roaches, mealworms and other smaller spiders. As well as pollen and nectar.
predator - spider wasps and mantises.
Lifespan - wild is 1 to 2 years, whereas, in captivity, it is 2 to 3

6.

Cosmophasis Umbratica (Shiny Jumping Spider)


Facts about the Cosmophasis Umbratica (Shiny Jumping Spider)

Other names: Green Iridescent Garden Jumper; Tropical Ornate Jumping Spider.
Distribution - found in South and Southeast Asia, tropical regions and India to Sumatra
Habitat - commonly spotted on green vegetation, low vegetation, plants, occasionally tree trunks. mainly spotted in regions with higher exposure to sunlight
Diet - mainly nectarivorous but also feeds off of insects, including flies and crickets.
Lifespan - 1-2 years Captive: 2-3 years.

7.

Hyllus Diardi (Heavy Jumper)


Facts about the Hyllus Diardi (heavy jumper)

Other Name - giant jumping spider
Distribution - India, Thailand, Indonesia, China, Laos, Myanmar, and Singapore
Habitat - Mangrove vegetation and waste-land
Diet - Smaller insects
Lifespan - wild: 1-2 years, captive: up to 3

8.

Portia Labiata (White-Moustached Jumping Spider)


Facts about the Portia Labiata (White-Moustached Jumping Spider)

Distribution - India, Burma, Sri Lanka, Burma, southern China, Singapore, Malaysia, Sumatra, Philippines, and Java
Habitat - Mostly in moist places
Diet - Web spiders, other jumping spider species and insects.
predator - Frogs, birds as well as certain insects such as mantises and bugs
Lifespan - Approximately 1.5 years
